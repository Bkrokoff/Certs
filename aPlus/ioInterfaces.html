I/O Interfaces (Motherboard)

Input / Output Panel:

The PS/2 is a 6pin - multi-din connector
Green PS/2: Used for plugging in the mouse
Purple PS/2: Used for plugging in the keyboard

USB stands for universal serial bus
Motherboards will typically have several USB ports
Also supplies electric power to that specific peripheral
USB versions: USB 1.0 - 1.5 Mb/s | USB 1.1 - 12 Mb/s | USB 2.0 - 480 Mb/s | USB 3.0 - 5 Gb/s

The Serial Port
An older technology rarely seen on motherboards today
Mainly used for connecting terminals and modems
The most common interface of a serial port is the RS-232 standard

The Parallel Port
Mainly used for connecting printers
Being phased out and replaced by the faster USB port
Uses a wide D-Sub connector known as the DB-25

The video port
Motherboards that do have a video adapter is known as integrated video
Generates images from your computer to your monitor
The most common kind of port on an integrated video adapter is VGA (Video Graphics Array)
The VGA port carries analog data. It has 15 pins divided into 3 rows and usually has a blue color
Integrated video adapters are usually not very powerful

The IEEE connector is commonly known as Firewire
Recognized by its "D" shape
Commonly used by attaching cameras or prinders
Transfer speed of 400 mb/s

The Network Interface Card (NIC) is used for netowrking purposes
Designed for an ethernet cable with a RJ-45 connector
Has its own unique identifier called a MAC address

The sound card
Processes audio through the computers speakers
Basic sound card will have a port for speakers (green | middle input) and a port for a microhpone (pink | bottom input)

The eSATA port is for attaching external SATA drives
Functions similar to USB and Firewire ports
Transfer speeds are faster, but require a separate power plug
The eSATAp port combines data transfer and power in an all-in-one port

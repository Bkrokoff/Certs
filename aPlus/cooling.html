Cooling

Cooling is very important to a computer
Computers generate a lot of heat
If the components are not adequately cooled the computer will: run slow, lock up, shut down, shorten the life of the computer
2 of the biggest heat generators come from the CPU and video card

Case Fans

Primary way of cooling
Intake fan - Brings air from outside the case into the case
Exhaust Fan - Pushes air outside the case
This known as ACTIVE COOLING

Heat Sink (CPU)

A heat sink is an aluminum block with fins that directly make contact with the CPU
Its purpose is to increase the surface area of the CPU so that it can make more air contact for cooling
The fins on the heat sink are designed to further increase the surface area for air circulation
Once the heat sink makes contact, the heat will transfer from the CPU to the heat sink

Thermal Compound

Used to fill in the microscopic air gaps between the heat sink and the CPU

Water Cooling

Water cooling is better than air cooling
They are quieter, but more expensive
Use a pump of constantly circulating water

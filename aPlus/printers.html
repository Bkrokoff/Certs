Printers

Printers allows the ability to print documents or pictures onto paper from a computer
The most common way a computer connects to a priter is by using a USB or parallel cable (local connection)
You can also connect a printer through a network
Network printers connect using an ethernet port or a wireless connection (network printers)

A NETWORK SHARE allows other computers on your network to connect using your local printer (sharing printer)
Your computer works as a gateway for other computers to connect
The computer that has the local connection to the printer must be turned on for other computers to use it

Types of Printers

Non-impact Printers

Print without striking an ink ribbon onto paper
Example: Laser, Inkjet, and Thermal Printers

Impact Printers

Dot Matrix Printers

Inkjet Printers

The most common printers that are used for home use
Print head moves back and forth across the paper during printing
Print head places ink on the paper in very tiny dots (Dots are so tiny they are smaller in diameter than a human hair)

Typically come with 2 ink cartiridges (1 for color, 1 for black)
More affordable than laser printers and produce photo quality results
Drawback: Ink may smudge, while ink from a laser printer does not smudge

Laser Printer

Laser printers come in different sizes
From small personal ones, to larger ones for businesses
Provide the highest quality print available
Most expensive printers

Basic operation:
Electric charge is placed on a rotating drum
Laser discharges a lower electrical charge on the drum
Laser draws the image that is going to be printed on the drum itself
Drum is then coated with a fine black powder, known as toner
Toner only clings to the areas where the laser has drawn
Toner is then placed on the paper, leading to a high quality print

Thermal Printer

Prints by using heat and uses a special paper called thermal paper
Thermal paper has wax based ink
As heat is applied to the ink, it turns black
Print head applies heat to the areas where the ink should be placed, when the ink is cooled it becomes permanent
Thermal printers are very quiet and are commonly used for printing labels and barcodes

Dot Matrix Printer

An old technology that produces mediocre print quality compared to inkjet and laser
They are also very noisy
The pins on the print head strike against a cloth ink ribbon that comes in direct contact with the paper
Characters are in the form of dots
Very durable and last a long time

Printer Issues

Streaks: Caused by inkjet cartridges
To fix: Use printer software tools to clean print head
Manually clean cartridges with a lint free cloth

Faded / Blank Image: Dirty ink cartidges or low on ink (Inkjet)
To fix: Replace ink cartridges

Ghosting: Faint image of a previous paint job (Laser printer)
Caused by: The drum - Drum in a laser printer has set lifespan after too many prints OR The Fuser
To fix: Replace them

Paper Jam: Could be caused by debris inside the printer
Debris could also cause paper to crease
Could be caused by faulty printer rollers
To fix: Replace rollers or clear debris

Color not printing correctly: Faulty color ink cartridge or low/out of colored ink
To fix: Replace cartridge

Nothing happens when you print: Could be caused by connectivity issue
Especially common with network printers
To fix: Check the obvious first - Printer is on | Check for errors on LCD display | Make sure network cable is plugged in (network printer)

Virtual printing

Instead of printing to paper you can print into a digital form on your computer or another computer

Print to file - allows you to print something in a printer file format (pm) so that you may use that file on another computer when you have access to a printer (Uneditable)
Print to PDF - Prints a document into a format that is readable across all devices (PDF) | Just about every new device on the market will be able to read a PDF
Older devices may need a pdf reader to read PDF's. The most common is Adobe Acrobat Reader (available to download for free)

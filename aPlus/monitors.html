Monitor

A computer monitor is the primary output component of a computer
Connects to the computers video adapter through a monitor cable and displays the images on the screen
A monitor may have a VGA, DVI, HDMI, or a DisplayPort

CRT Monitor

CRT (Cathode Ray Tube) was the technology first used in tv sets
Uses 3 color electron guns (red, blue, green)
Constantly redrawing the image on the screen
These guns sweep across the face of the monitor many times per second, known as the REFRESH RATE
A refresh rate of 60 hz = redraws the image 60 times per second

Inside a CRT monitor is a metal plate called a shadow mask
Shadow mask can pick up outside external magnetic fields which cause discoloration
DEGAUSSING is the process of getting rid of the unwanted magnetic fields

LCD Monitor

LCD (liquid Crystal Display) monitor, also known as flat panel displays
Produce an image on a flat surface by using liquid crystals
Light shines through these crystals to produce an image on the screen
They are lighter, thinner, use less power, and take up less space than CRT monitors
TFT (Thin Film Transistor) is used to create a higher image quality
Each pixel on the screen has its own transistor

Two Types of LCD Monitors: TN vs IPS

Twisted Nematic

Liquid Crystals twist the light as it comes through
Older and cheaper technology
Colors and viewing angles are not as good as modern LCDs

In-Plane Switching

Liquid crystals stay in place and are aligned in parallel with the glass
Uses more trnasistors and consumes more power
Better color reproduction and viewing angles

LCD Monitor Light Sources: Fluorescent vs LED

Fluorescent

Uses fluorescent lamps for backlighting
Emits ultraviolet light when the mercury vapor inside the lamp is ionized

LED

Uses multiple LEDs (light emitting diodes) arranged in a pattern
Modern and newer form of backlighting

Both of these are considered LCD monitors, but LCD that uses LED backlighting is considered an LED monitor

Resolution

Resolution is defined as the number of pixels that are used to display an image on the screen
For example: 1280 x 720 = 921600 pixels being used
The higher the resolution, the higher the number of pixels being used
LCDs produce their best quality image when they are set to their highest resolution, which is called their NATIVE RESOLUTION

Resolutions are given different names for identity purposes:
XGA (extended graphics array) 1028 x 768
SXGA (super extended graphics array) 1280 x 1024
SXGA+ (super extended graphics array plus) 1400 x 1050
UXGA (ultra extended graphics array) 1600 x 1200
WUXGA (wide-screen ultra extended graphics array) 1920 x 1200

The CONTRAST RATIO refers to the contrast between black and white
The higher the contrast, the better the display

Adapter / Expansion Cards

Circuit boards that can be installed into the expanion slots of the motherboard
Adapter card are designed to increase the functionality of the computer
For example: Video cards, audio cards, storage cards, etc

Video Card

Generates images from the computer to the monitor
Also known as graphics card, graphics adapters, etc
Has several components: GPU, Memory, Bus Type, and video ports
Video is transferred to the monitor through the video ports

Video Ports

S-video(Separate Video or Super Video) is an analog transmitter
Transmits 2 signals over one cable - Color and brightness
Round port - black color

VGA (Video Graphics Array)
Older technology - developed in 1987
Has 15 pins - divided into 3 rows
Blue color
Carries analog data

DVI (Digital Visual Interface)
Newer technology designed to succeed the VGA port
Developed in 1999
Desiged to deliver uncompressed high quality video to LCD monitors
DVI-A - Used to send analog signals only | DVI-D - Used to send digital signals only | DVI-I - Used to send both analog and digital signals

HDMI (High Definition Multimedia Interface)
Developed in 2002
Designed to deliver uncompessed video and audio data through a single cable

Display Port
Debuted in 2006
Developed by VESA (Video Electronics Standards Association)
Primarily used for video, but can also be used to carry USB and audio data as well

Sound Card

Processes audio through the computers speakers
Has an audio (green) output port for attaching speakers
Has an input (pink) port for a microphone

Firewire Card

Adapted card with firewire ports
Adds or expands firewire capability to a computer

USB card

Adapter card with USB ports
Adds or expands USB capability to a computer

RAID Card

Adapter card for creating a hardware RAID array
RAID is used for data redundancy
In a hardware RAID setup, the operating system is not aware of RAID implementation

eSATA card

Used for adding external SATA ports
Adds the capability of adding extra SATA hard drives
Adding extra hard drives is primarily used for backing up and storing data

Video Capture Card

Allows a user to capture analog video, for example from a video camera and then convert it to a digital form and then store it on their computer

TV Tuner Card

Allows signals from a television to be picked up by a computer

NIC (Network Interface Card)

Used to connect a computer to a network using an ethernet cable
Converts serial data into parallel data
Provides a constant dedicated connection to a network
Each NIC has its own unique identifier, called a MAC address

Wireless NIC
Connects to a network wirelessly
Has a built in antenna
Convenient way for computers to connect to a network without the hassle of cables

PCMCIA Card

A PC card is an expansion card for laptop computers
Originally called a PCMCIA (Personal Computer Memory Card International Assocation) card
Slides into the external slot giving the laptop added capability

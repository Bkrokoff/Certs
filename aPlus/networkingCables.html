Networking Cables & Connectors

RJ-11 Connector

The RJ-11 (registered jack) is a telephone jack
Used to connect to modems on computers
Has a hinged locking tab that locks itself in place
Resembles the RJ-45 but is smaller

RJ-45 Connector

Most common network connector
Used with twisted pair cabling
Used to attach network interface cards to hubs or switches
Resembles the RJ-11 but is larger

BNC Connector

Common type of RF connector
Bayonet Neill-Concelman
Used for both analog and digital video as well as audio

F-Type Connector

A screw on connector used on coaxial cables
Used today primarily to cable providers to attach to cable modems
Hand tightens by an attached nut

IEEE 1394 Firewire

Recognized by its "D" shape
Commonly associated with attaching peripheral devices, such as digital cameras and printers than network connections
Found on many types of video and multimedia devices

USB (Universal Serial Bus)

Very common on desktops and laptops
Just about every kind of peripheral connects to a computer using a USB connection
Examples: Type A, Type B, Mini, Micro

Thunderbolt Connector

High speed technology interface that outputs one serial signal from a combination of PCI express and the display port
Released in 2011
Mainly used in Apple products
Version 1 & 2 uses mini display port | Version 3 uses USB type-C

RCA Connector

Older connector produced in 1940s
Primarily used to carry video and audio signals
Red - Audio | White - Audio | Yellow - Video

ST Connector

Fiber optic cable connector
Uses a half-twist bayonet type of lock
Commonly used with single-mode fiber optic cable

LC Connector

The LC (Local Connector) is a fiber optic connector
Jack is similar to the RJ-45 style
Commonly used between floors on a building

SC Connector

The SC (Standard Connector) is a fiber optic cable connector
Uses a push-pull connector similar to audio and video plugs
Commonly used between floors on a building

Plenum vs Non-Plenum Cables

Plenum spaces means open air-flow
Non-plenum spaces means they use air ducts
Therefore plenum spaces are more prone to fires, so plenum cables must meet certain requirements

Plenum Cables
Must not produce toxic fumes if they are burned

Ethernet over powerline

Sometimes there might be cases in your home or office where you wanted a certain computer in a certain part of the building, to be able to access the internet or to be networked
And for whatever reason, network cabling or wifi wasnt an option in that part of the building
Ethernet over power line gives this ability of ethernet networking over power
Powerline Network Adapter - One plugs into the wall near the computer and one plugs in near the modem - They connect via the power lines

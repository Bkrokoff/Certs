Motherboard Form Factor

Motherboard - Main component of a computer
A large circuit board where all the computer components connect to:
Processor socket - where the CPU is placed
Memory Slots - where the RAM goes
Bus slots - used to install various components to add more capabilties to the computer such as video card, sound card, network card, etc

Form Factor (Motherboards)

ATX (Advanced Technology Extended) is the most common form factor motherboard used in PCs today
The de facto standard form factor for PCs today
ATX motherboard are 12 inches by 9.6 inches

AT was the form factor that was used in the 1980s that was developed by IBM
AT motherboards are 12 inches by 13.8 inches in size
No longer in development

Micro ATX boards are smaller than ATX boards
9.6 inches by 9.6 inches
Designed to fit in smaller computer cases and is cheaper. Fewer fatures and less power than standard ATX board

BTX form factor was designed by intel to make further improvements from the ATX
Improved board design, which creates a more in line air flow
Flexible enough to work both in large and small cases

ITX was designed for smaller, space saving computers
Consume less power and are cooled by the use of heat sinks (no fans)
Designed by VIA technologies
Mini - ITX | Nano - ITX | Pico - ITX | Mobile - ITX (6cm x 6cm)

NLX was created by Intel and it was designed for low-end / low profile computers
Utilizes a riser card
Expansion cards can plug in parallel with the motherboard

WAN Termination

If you are using services from a WAN provider or ISP, there needs to be a physical location that designates the connection point between the providers network and your internal network (Demarcation Point)

Demarcation Point

The point where you connect to the outside world
WAN provider
Internet Service Provider
The demarc

The demarcation point is used everywhere
Could be connected to your home

It is in a central location when in buildings
Usually a network interface device
Can be as simple as an RJ-45 connection

The location of a demarc is important - especially when problems occurring
You can understand whether the problem is on the provider side or your side of the demarc

Usually if the problem is on your side, you have your own equipment and your own routers on your own premises

This is referred to as CPE (Customer Premises Equipment)

Smartjack

Providers will put specialized equipment in your demarc room that they are able to control remotely - smartjack

Technically it is a NIU (Network INterface Unit)

This is a powered system that is monitoring and is able to provide diagnostics remotely, so the provider can connect to the smartjack, run their own tests, and determine if the problems are occuring on the providers side or your side of the network
